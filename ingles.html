Solid Waste Separation

Solid waste separation is the process of sorting trash based on its type or composition for proper management. It is typically done at home, school, or work, and is the first step toward correct waste handling.

Hazardous Waste

These are wastes that can be toxic or polluting.

  • Examples: batteries, expired medications, oils, chemicals, electronics.
  • Use: require special handling by authorities or certified companies.

Non-Recyclable Inorganics

Items that are difficult or impossible to recycle.

  • Examples: diapers, used toilet paper, cigarette butts, dirty wrappers.
  • Use: final disposal in landfills.

Recyclable Inorganics

Materials that can be reused or recycled.

  • Examples: plastic, glass, paper, cardboard, metals.
  • Use: industrial recycling.

Organic Waste

Biodegradable waste from living beings.

  • Examples: food scraps, fruit peels, leaves, dried flowers.
  • Use: composting or natural fertilizer production.

Properly separating waste facilitates recycling, reduces pollution, and minimizes the amount of trash ending up in landfills. It also encourages a culture of environmental responsibility and improves quality of life for current and future generations.
